Unleash Your Geek 2.0: Low-Cost Sensors to COmbat Flooding

Working with local universities to prepare for natural disasters
USER-FRIENDLY CITY

What's the problem we're solving?

The sudden and destructive flooding that occurred in February 2017 was the worst San Jose has seen in decades, resulting in the emergency evacuation of approximately 14,000 residents overnight. As extreme weather incidences make flooding more likely, San Jose needs a more responsive and accurate flood detection system to ensure residents are evacuated in a timely and safe manner. The call to action for Unleash Your Geek 2.0 arose in response to needs that were identified by the City and Water District following the destructive flooding and slow emergency response to it. To address these issues, student teams from local universities are collaborating directly with emergency responders and other key stakeholders within the city to design a flood sensing system that allows for quicker communication between the Water District, relevant City departments, and residents.

What's the problem we're solving?

A joint effort between Santa Clara University’s Frugal Innovation Hub and San Jose State University’s Robotics, Sensors, and Machine Intelligence Lab is being undertaken to address some of these challenges. The joint student team is collaborating closely with the City and Water District to create a smart flood detection sensor that not only measures water height more accurately than the more expensive sensors currently deployed on City bridges, but which is AI-enabled to capture and classify images of water and debris-flows to build a better, more encompassing flood-monitoring system.

Our Impact

The 2017 San Jose floods led to catastrophic loss of property and disarray as residents scrambled to find shelter and safety. An early warning flood monitoring system will not only prevent and mitigate future loss, but allow for the City and Water District to gain key insights about its waterways and natural landscapes through the image-capture and analytical functions of their partnership with the SCU-SJSU student team

Thank you to the Frugal Innovation Hub at Santa Clara University’s School of Engineering (Behnam Dezfouli, Allan Baez Morales, and student engineers) and San Jose State University (Winncy Du and student engineers)for your ongoing efforts to develop and deploy a smart flood detection sensor to prevent destructive flooding in San Jose.
