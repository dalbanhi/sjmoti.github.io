My San Jose app

Making service requests a snap
USER-FRIENDLY CITY

One of our dreams is for city services to to be as easy as ordering packing tape from Amazon Prime. We’re a ways away from getting there, but the My San Jose app is a significant step in the right direction. As smartphones become pervasive, we knew we needed an app for residents to easily ask the city to fix potholes or pick up a stray mattress from the sidewalk.

One of the decisions we made early on was to not take the easy route of simply building an email service where user requests get sent into an inbox. Instead, we engineered our service request process so that requests are directly routed into the workflow of service teams helping us to improve our service and speed response times.

Since launching the app in August 2017, we’ve processed tens of thousands of service requests. Our work is nowhere close to being done though. As with any good software product, we must continue to iterate and improve on the product with the help of our users.

Thanks to UChicago’s Data Science for Social Good team, Code Enforcement’s Mollie McLeod, and San Jose’s Data Analytics Lead Erica Garaffo for making this project a success.
